Sally Ride

On June 18, Ride became the first American woman in space, aboard the space shuttle Challenger.

In 1983, astronaut and astrophysicist Sally Ride became the first American woman in space aboard the space shuttle Challenger. Ride died on July 23, 2012 at the age of 61, following a battle with pancreatic cancer.

  1. Sally Ride grew up in Los Angeles and went to Stanford University, where she was a double major in physics and English.
  2. Ride beat out 1,000 other applicants for a spot in the National Aeronautics and Space Administration's (NASA) astronaut program.
  3. After NASA, Ride became the director of the California Space Institute at the University of California, San Diego.
  4. Ride received many honors, including the NASA Space Flight Medal and the NCAA's Theodore Roosevelt Award.
